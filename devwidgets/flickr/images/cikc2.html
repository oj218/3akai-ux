CIKC Cambridge Integrated Knowledge center

Advanced Manufacturing Technologies for photonics and electronics -

Exploiting molecular and macromolecular technologies

The Cambridge Integrated Knowledge Centre (CIKC) is a centre of excellence for low temperature processing of macromolecular materials, such as polymers, liquid crystals and nanostructures, for applications in computers, displays and communications.

CIKC brings together world-class research activities in Cambridge University in molecular and macromolecular materials in the Electrical Engineering Division and the Cavendish Laboratory with the expertise of the Judge Business School, the Institute for Manufacturing (IfM) and the Centre for Business Research (CBR) and industry partners to accelerate the commercialisation of emerging technology.

CIKC technologies include:

  • inkjet-printed poylmer TFTs
  • roll-to-roll printing of organic solar cells,
  • transparent inorganic microelectronics,
  • LCOS devices for holographic projection,
  • low cost polymer photonic components
  • large area liquid crystal displays on plastic.

The Mission of the Centre is to provide the business and technical expertise and infrastructure to enable those with exploitable concepts to achieve commercial success
